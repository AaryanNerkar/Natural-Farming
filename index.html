Frequently Asked Questions

What is natural farming? add

Natural farming is an ecological farming approach that works in harmony with nature. It avoids synthetic fertilizers and pesticides, focusing on building healthy soil ecosystems to produce nutritious food.

How can I start practicing natural farming? add

You can start by creating your own compost, using natural pest repellents, and learning about companion planting. We offer workshops and resources to guide you.

What are the benefits of natural farming? add

The benefits are numerous: improved soil health, better water conservation, higher nutritional value in food, reduced costs for farmers, and a healthier environment for everyone.

How does my donation help? add

Your donations directly fund our awareness programs, support farmer training, help develop educational materials, and enable us to reach more communities to spread the message of natural farming.

Discover Natural Farming

Learn more about the principles and advantages of adopting a natural approach to agriculture for a healthier planet and healthier you.

Core Principles

Natural farming is built on simple yet powerful principles: no tilling, no chemical fertilizers, no pesticides, and no weeding. It's about letting nature do what it does best, with minimal human intervention.

Benefits for All

This method restores soil health, increases biodiversity, conserves water, and produces nutrient-dense food. Farmers benefit from lower costs, and consumers enjoy healthier produce. It's a win-win for people and the planet.
