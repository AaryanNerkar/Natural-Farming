The Journey of Yash Solanki

Yash and Neel Solanki, from Garade village near Saswad, Maharashtra, began natural farming in 2020 during the COVID-19 lockdown. Neel studied Organic Farming in the USA, while Yash pursued Climate Science and Policy. Despite lucrative opportunities abroad, they returned to India to dedicate themselves to farming and reviving sustainable traditions.

Embracing Natural Farming

Along with farming, they produce handmade masalas such as Chai Masala, Peri-Peri Masala, Pav Bhaji Masala, Sambar Masala, and Maharaniâ€™s Sabzi Masala. These are made from naturally grown crops, produced in small batches, chemical-free, and preservative-free. Though priced higher, they reflect true natural value. Neel even declined a lucrative job to remain committed to full-time natural farming.

Achievements and Techniques

Their philosophy focuses on working closely with soil and nature. They avoid chemical fertilizers and pesticides, nurture soil health organically, and promote biodiversity. Farming for them is not just livelihood, but a lifestyle rooted in authenticity, purity, and trust.

Inspiring Others

The Solanki brothers encourage youth to pursue meaningful work, adopt sustainable farming, and bring eco-friendly approaches even into corporate life. Their journey shows that farming is about nurturing society, environment, and future generations, proving how passion and education can create impactful change.
