The Journey of Madhav Ghule

Madhav Ghule, from Manjri, Maharashtra, has been practicing natural farming since 2012–2013. His turning point came after attending a shibir (training camp) where he met Pratibha Tai, who guided him. With her support, Madhav and fellow farmers formed a collective, moving from selling vegetables on the roadside to gaining recognition for natural farming.

Embracing Natural Farming

He completely avoids chemicals and synthetic fertilizers, instead preparing natural fertilizers at home using neem leaves, gomutra (cow urine), and cow dung. His focus is on producing and delivering vishmukt (toxin-free) food to society.

Achievements and Techniques

Madhav mainly grows Deshi Khapli Gahu, an indigenous wheat variety with only 0.02% gluten, safe for diabetic patients. He has also expanded into value-added products such as bread, pizza bases, cookies, and toasts. This shows how natural farming can also support innovation and income generation.

  • Natural Farming Journey: Started in 2012–2013; guided by Pratibha Tai and formed a farmers’ collective for recognition.
  • Farming Practices: Avoids chemicals, prepares natural fertilizers using neem, gomutra, and cow dung.
  • Crops & Products: Grows Deshi Khapli Gahu (0.02% gluten) and produces bread, pizza bases, cookies, and toasts.
  • Message to Youth: Promotes toxin-free food, respect for nature, and investment in health through natural farming.

Inspiring Others

Alongside his role as a Product Manager at Siemens, he continues part-time farming, living by his motto: “Delivering toxin-free food to people.” His message to youth is clear—health is wealth, avoid chemical food, respect nature, and invest in natural produce instead of medical expenses.
